Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to commonly asked questions about the ATLAS tool.

click sections to expand

Why do we need ATLAS?

Addiction is a chronic disease and can be treated effectively. But the use of treatments proven to work varies greatly among treatment facilities. Many areas of health care have indicators (measures of a given health topic), rating systems, and websites that bring transparency and information on treatment quality to the public. But there is no such system for addiction treatment. This information is vital to helping individuals or their loved ones make informed decisions about the best care to meet their needs. To fill this gap, ATLAS aims to:

  • Develop quality standards for addiction treatment facilities across:
    • all treatment settings—such as outpatient, inpatient, and residential; and
    • different types of treatment—such as behavioral therapy and medication.
  • Promote the use of treatments that are shown to work.
  • Track and support quality improvement in addiction treatment.

How is ATLAS different from other websites and tools?

Some tools can help you find addiction treatment facilities based on your location and your personal preferences or needs, such as your insurance plan or the specific substance misused. But ATLAS is the first and only tool that also assesses and displays measures of quality care for addiction. This allows you to can compare facilities and choose the one that meets your needs without guessing if the facility is offering the right kind of care.
